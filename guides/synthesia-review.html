What Is Synthesia?

Synthesia is a platform for creating AI video with virtual presenters. Instead of filming a real person, you choose an avatar, write a script, and Synthesia generates a video of that avatar delivering your content.

The primary use case is corporate training videos. Instead of booking a film crew, talent, and studio, you can create professional-looking training content in hours. This is genuinely valuable for organizations that need to produce lots of localized content.

I've tested Synthesia for creating training materials, product demos, and multilingual content. Here's the honest assessment.

Avatar Quality

The first question everyone asks: do the avatars look real?

Short answer: no, not close-up and not if you're looking carefully. But the quality has improved significantly.

Stock Avatars: Synthesia offers 140+ pre-built avatars across diverse demographics. These range from photorealistic to clearly synthetic. The better ones are usable in corporate contexts; the weaker ones are clearly AI-generated.

Custom Avatars: You can create an avatar that looks like a specific person (with consent). This requires filming a person reading scripts for several hours. The resulting avatar can then say anything you type. Quality is better than stock avatars but still has that "uncanny valley" quality.

What works: Medium shots, corporate/professional contexts, narration-style content. The avatar points at slides, makes appropriate gestures, and reads clearly.

What doesn't work: Close-ups, emotional content, anything requiring subtle facial expressions. The synthetic nature becomes obvious.

Multi-Language Support

This is Synthesia's strongest feature. You can create videos in 130+ languages with appropriate accents and lip-sync.

Write your script in English, select "Spanish," and get a Spanish-speaking avatar. The lip-sync is generally good, and the language pronunciation has improved dramatically from early versions.

For organizations needing to localize training content, this is transformative. Create content once, distribute in dozens of languages, without hiring translators or re-filming.

The quality varies by language. Common languages (Spanish, French, German, Mandarin) work better than less common ones. Always review outputs, especially for critical content.

Key Features

Video Editor

Synthesia has a built-in editor for composing videos:

  • Add slides and screens alongside the avatar
  • Adjust avatar position and size
  • Add text overlays and graphics
  • Customize backgrounds
  • Include AI-generated B-roll

The editor is intuitive for basic video composition. For complex productions, you'll want additional editing software.

AI Script Assistant

Synthesia includes AI assistance for writing scripts. Given a topic, it can generate a draft script optimized for the platform. Useful for getting started or overcoming writer's block.

Video Templates

Pre-built templates for common use cases:

  • Training course intro
  • Product announcement
  • Compliance training
  • Welcome message

Templates speed up production by providing a starting structure.

Use Cases

Corporate Training

Synthesia's sweet spot. Training content often involves factual information delivered by a professional. The avatar provides consistency, and you can easily update content without re-filming.

Example: A software company creates product training for 20 languages. Instead of 20 separate video productions, they create master content and localize with Synthesia.

Compliance and Policy Updates

When policies change, quickly update video content. This is faster and cheaper than re-filming.

Customer Education

Some companies use Synthesia for product tutorials and how-to content. The avatar adds a human element without production costs.

What to Avoid

  • High-emotion content
  • Content requiring subtle expressions
  • Marketing content where authenticity matters
  • Anything where the synthetic nature would undermine the message

Final Verdict

Synthesia isn't trying to replace Hollywood productions. It's solving a specific problem: creating video content at scale without production overhead.

For corporate training, internal communications, and localization, it delivers genuine value. The cost savings compared to traditional video production are real, even if the quality isn't equivalent.

For organizations producing lots of training content across languages, Synthesia pays for itself quickly. For occasional use, the economics are less compelling.

My rating: 7.8/10. Niche value proposition, but delivers for its target use cases.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I create my own avatar?

Yes, Synthesia offers custom avatar creation. This requires filming a person (with consent) reading scripts for several hours. The resulting avatar can say anything. Custom avatars are available on Enterprise plans.

How many languages does Synthesia support?

Synthesia supports 130+ languages with appropriate lip-sync and accents. Common languages have better quality than less common ones.

Can I use Synthesia videos commercially?

Yes, videos created with Synthesia can be used commercially. Review their Terms of Service for specific restrictions and consent requirements for custom avatars.

What's the video quality?

Synthesia outputs at up to 1080p. Quality is good for corporate and training content but not broadcast quality.

Can I edit videos after generating?

Limited editing. You can adjust timing, add slides/graphics, and change backgrounds. For significant edits, regenerate the video.

Is Synthesia better than traditional video?

No—but it's cheaper and faster for certain use cases. For corporate training and localization, Synthesia offers better economics. For high-quality marketing content, traditional production is still necessary.

How long does video generation take?

Generation time varies by length and complexity. A 5-minute video typically takes 15-30 minutes to generate. Priority processing is available on higher plans.
