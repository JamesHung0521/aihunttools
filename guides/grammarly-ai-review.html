Grammarly's Evolution

Grammarly started as a grammar checker—a useful but limited tool for catching typos and run-on sentences. In 2023-2024, it pivoted hard into AI-powered writing assistance, adding features like tone adjustment, AI rewriting, and clarity enhancement.

The question now isn't just "is Grammarly good at grammar?" but "is Grammarly worth it as an AI writing tool?" The answer is nuanced.

Grammarly is not trying to replace Jasper or Copy.ai as a content generation tool. Instead, it positions itself as "writing enhancement"—improving what you write rather than writing for you. This is a smart differentiation.

Grammar and Spelling

Grammarly's bread and butter. This is where it started, and it remains excellent.

I tested with deliberately error-riddled text:

  • "Their going to the store tommorow to by sum things for they're new house."

Grammarly caught all errors and offered correct suggestions. Commas, homophones, subject-verb agreement—all identified and fixed. This is table stakes for Grammarly, and it delivers.

Beyond basics, Grammarly handles:

  • Passive voice (when appropriate)
  • Sentence structure issues
  • Punctuation errors
  • Wordiness
  • Readability issues

The suggestions are explainable. Hover over a suggestion, and you get a brief explanation of the rule. This helps users learn rather than just fix.

Tone and Clarity

Newer features that came with Grammarly's AI pivot.

Tone Detection

Grammarly analyzes your writing and assigns a tone—confident, friendly, formal, etc. I wrote a casual email to a colleague, and Grammarly correctly identified "friendly and professional."

The value: catching mismatches. If you're trying for professional but your tone reads as aggressive, Grammarly flags it.

Clarity Score

Grammarly provides an overall clarity score for your document. My test document scored 85/100 with suggestions to improve. The suggestions were actionable: break up long sentences, use more active voice, simplify complex phrases.

Delivery Insights

For emails, Grammarly offers "delivery insights" suggesting politeness strategies, question framing, and call-to-action optimization. This is genuinely useful for business communication.

AI Rewriting Features

Grammarly's AI features under the "Compass" and "Rewrite" umbrella:

Generate Alternative Drafts

Highlight text, click "Rewrite," and Grammarly offers alternative versions. I tested with a paragraph of mediocre writing and got three improved variations. The quality was decent—not as polished as what ChatGPT produces, but good enough to save time.

Adjust Tone

Take formal text, ask for "more casual," get a casual rewrite. The tone shifting works reasonably well. The casual rewrite of my formal email retained the core message while lightening the tone.

Simplify or Expand

Simplify complex text for easier reading, or expand sparse text with more detail. Both work, though simplification sometimes over-simplifies technical content.

Pros and Cons Lists

Generate structured lists from descriptions. I described a product and asked for pros and cons; Grammarly produced a reasonable list. Useful for comparison content.

Plagiarism Detection

Included in Premium, plagiarism detection compares your text against billions of web pages and academic papers.

I tested with a paragraph of clearly plagiarized content. Grammarly identified the source with 100% confidence. I also tested with heavily paraphrased content—Grammarly still caught the similarity in most cases.

This feature is essential for:

  • Academic papers
  • Professional submissions
  • Content agencies ensuring originality
  • Anyone concerned about accidental plagiarism

The database isn't perfect—some niche sources might be missed—but it's comprehensive enough for most use cases.

Pricing Breakdown

Plan Price Key Features
Free $0 Grammar, spelling, punctuation, basic tone
Premium $12/month ($144/year) Everything + AI features, plagiarism, tone customization
Business $15/user/month Team admin, brand tones, prioritized support

The Free tier is genuinely useful. If you just need grammar checking, you don't need to pay.

Premium at $144/year is expensive for individual users. The AI features are nice but not revolutionary. The value depends on how much you write and how much you need tone adjustment and plagiarism checking.

Business at $15/user/month makes more sense for teams where consistency matters.

Final Verdict

Grammarly remains the best grammar checker available. The browser extension integration means it works everywhere—Gmail, Google Docs, Slack, everywhere. That's powerful.

The AI features are genuine additions, not just marketing. The tone adjustment and clarity suggestions improve writing. But they're not transformative enough to justify Premium for everyone.

My recommendation: use the Free tier for grammar checking. If you write professionally and need tone consistency or plagiarism checking, Premium might be worth it.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Grammarly worth it in 2025?

For grammar checking alone, the Free tier is excellent. Premium's value depends on how much you need AI rewriting, tone adjustment, and plagiarism detection.

Does Grammarly work on all platforms?

Grammarly works via browser extension (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge), desktop app, keyboard app (iOS/Android), and integrations with Google Docs, Microsoft Office, and more. Not perfect coverage, but extensive.

Can Grammarly generate content?

Grammarly can rewrite and adjust existing content, but it's not designed for content generation like Jasper or ChatGPT. It's a writing enhancement tool, not a content creation tool.

How accurate is plagiarism detection?

Grammarly's plagiarism checker is comprehensive and accurate for most use cases. It compares against billions of web pages and academic papers. Very niche sources might be missed, but overall it's reliable.

Can I use Grammarly for academic writing?

Yes, many students and academics use Grammarly. The plagiarism detection is particularly valuable for academic work. Some institutions have concerns about AI writing assistance—check your school's policies.

What's the difference between Grammarly and ChatGPT?

Grammarly focuses on improving existing writing (grammar, tone, clarity). ChatGPT generates new content. They can work together—generate with ChatGPT, refine with Grammarly.

Does Grammarly have team features?

Yes, the Business plan ($15/user/month) includes team admin controls, brand tones, style guides, and centralized billing. Good for organizations needing writing consistency.
