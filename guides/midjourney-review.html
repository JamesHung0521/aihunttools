What Is Midjourney?

Midjourney is an AI image generator that exploded in popularity in 2022. It creates images from text descriptions, or prompts, and has become known for its artistic, often surreal aesthetic. Unlike competitors that started with web interfaces, Midjourney built its community on Discord first—a choice that shaped its user experience in ways both good and bad.

I've been using Midjourney since version 4, watching it evolve through multiple model iterations. Version 6, released in late 2023 and refined since, brought significant improvements in prompt following, image coherence, and text rendering. The question in 2025 is whether Midjourney still leads the pack or if competitors like DALL-E 3 and Stable Diffusion have caught up.

V6 Capabilities Deep Dive

Let me break down what V6 actually brings to the table:

Prompt Understanding

V6 understands natural language better than previous versions. You can write relatively casual prompts and get results that match your intent. A prompt like "an elderly fisherman at dawn, golden light, weathered face, peaceful expression, cinematic" produces coherent results that honor each element.

More complex compositional requests work better too. "A bustling Renaissance market seen from ground level, with merchants selling exotic goods, customers haggling, children playing, afternoon light streaming through canvas awnings" produces images with the depth and activity described.

Image Quality

Midjourney images have a distinctive look. They're often described as painterly or cinematic, with rich colors and dramatic lighting. This aesthetic isn't neutral—images have personality. Whether that's a pro or con depends on what you're creating.

For artistic work, concept art, and illustrations, Midjourney excels. For photorealism, it's good but not always the best choice. Stable Diffusion with the right model often wins for hyperrealistic output.

Text Rendering

Text rendering was notoriously bad in earlier versions. V6 improved this significantly. I tested prompts like "a poster that says AI ART" and got readable text most of the time. Not perfect, but usable in many contexts. For production work requiring clean text, you'll still want to composite in design software.

Real-World Testing

Let me share specific results from my testing:

Portrait Photography Style

Prompt: "Editorial portrait of a woman in her 40s, professional lighting, natural makeup, neutral background, fashion magazine style"

Results were impressive. The faces looked human without the uncanny valley issues that plagued earlier AI generators. Skin textures were realistic, lighting was professional-grade. One image would pass in a stock photography portfolio.

Fantasy Landscape

Prompt: "Ancient floating islands in a sunset sky, waterfalls cascading into clouds, bioluminescent plants, epic scale, fantasy art style"

Classic Midjourney territory. The output was breathtaking—exactly the kind of concept art you'd see in a AAA video game pitch. Rich detail, cohesive world-building, dramatic composition.

Product Photography

Prompt: "Minimalist product shot of a ceramic coffee mug on a marble surface, soft shadows, clean background, commercial photography"

This was mixed. The images looked artistic but occasionally struggled with the "product photography" precision needed. Shadows were sometimes too dramatic, reflections not quite right. Fine for mood boards; not quite production-ready without editing.

Architectural Visualization

Prompt: "Modern sustainable architecture, green roof garden, natural materials, residential home, late afternoon light"

Architectural prompts work well for concept exploration. The buildings looked plausible, materials read correctly. Not a replacement for CAD or rendering software, but excellent for early-stage ideation.

The Discord Experience

Midjourney's Discord-first approach is... unique. On one hand, it's created a thriving community where you can see what others are creating, get inspired, and learn from prompts. The /describe feature (which reverse-engineers prompts from images) is incredibly useful.

On the other hand, Discord is not designed for image work. Managing generations, comparing variations, downloading files—it's all more cumbersome than a dedicated web app. You're essentially using a chat platform as an image generation tool.

Midjourney recently launched a web interface (alpha.midjourney.com) which is more usable for core generation work. But it's still limited compared to what you can do in Discord. Hopefully, the web experience continues improving.

How It Compares to Alternatives

See my Midjourney vs DALL-E 3 comparison for a detailed head-to-head. The short version: Midjourney wins on artistic quality and community; DALL-E 3 wins on prompt precision and web interface.

Stable Diffusion remains the free option with more customization potential, but requires more technical skill to use effectively.

Final Verdict

Midjourney V6 remains one of the best AI art generators available. The image quality is consistently impressive, the community is valuable, and regular improvements keep it competitive.

But the Discord-first workflow frustrates many users, and competitors have caught up in many areas. If you prioritize artistic quality and don't mind the Discord interface, Midjourney is still worth it.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Midjourney free to use?

There's a one-time free trial offering 25 images. After that, paid plans start at $10/month for the Basic tier. No free tier beyond the trial.

What does fast generation mean?

Midjourney queues can get long during busy periods. Fast generation gives you priority processing. Relaxed mode puts you in a lower-priority queue but doesn't consume your fast hours.

Can I use Midjourney images commercially?

Yes, images created with Midjourney can be used commercially under most subscription plans. Check their current Terms of Service for specific restrictions.

How does Midjourney V6 compare to V5.2?

V6 has better prompt following, improved text rendering, more coherent compositions, and better facial rendering. The visual style is similar but refined.

Is Midjourney or DALL-E 3 better?

It depends on your needs. See our Midjourney vs DALL-E 3 comparison for details. Generally, Midjourney is better for artistic/conceptual work; DALL-E 3 is better for precise prompt matching and web integration.

Can Midjourney generate photorealistic images?

Yes, but with caveats. It can produce photorealistic images, though some competitors may edge it out for pure realism. Midjourney tends toward a more artistic/painterly aesthetic.

What are --style raw and other parameters?

Parameters like --style raw reduce Midjourney's default stylization for more literal interpretation. Others include --ar for aspect ratio, --chaos for variation, --s for stylize strength. The documentation explains all available options.
